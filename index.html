Born and raised in the warm embrace of Southern California, I grew amongst my latino brothers and sisters. We played on the asphalt scraping up our knees as the cans and bottles of modelo from the local gang glistened in the bright sunshine of another spring school day, we worked in the backyards of our middle class schoolmates two story homes, but most importantly we struggled together in the classroom. Standardized tests, the fact that most of our parents never finished middle school and the ultimate truth that traditional public education wasn't designed for us. This is what pushed me to pursue an education in economics. Education is the one and only true equalizer of men and women, but our people need somewhere to get their footing economically. My purpose, I believe, is to stand as a pillar for those who look like me and not only guide them through the system, but to also find solutions to the economic scarcity and fulfill their financial needs.
